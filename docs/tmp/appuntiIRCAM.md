## 1. IRCAM COME ISTITUZIONE CENTRALIZZATA E VERTICALIZZATA

### 1.1 Struttura di potere e gerarchia
**Capitolo 4 - The Institution of IRCAM: Culture and Status (pp. 102-142)**

- **Organigramma formale vs realtà informale** (pp. 106-107, 143-144)
  - Struttura burocratica apparente con dipartimenti autonomi
  - Realtà: potere concentrato nel Comitato Artistico presieduto da Boulez

  - Riproduzione: amministrazione, servizi, segreteria (basso status)
  - Divisione sessuale e razziale del lavoro netta
  - Gerarchia interna nella sfera di produzione

## 4. DIVISIONE DEL LAVORO E GERARCHIE DI STATUS

### 4.1 Compositori vs Tutors
**Capitolo 8 - A Composer's Visit (pp. 223-251)**
**Capitolo 9 - Aporias: Technological and Social Problems (pp. 262-269)**

- **Processo di commissione** (pp. 113-114)
  - 2 visite di 3 mesi: "research" + "production"
  - Fee moderati (20.000-35.000 franchi per 6+ mesi lavoro)
  - Trattamento differenziato per "star composers"
  - Assistenza da tutors/junior tutors

- **Il ruolo del tutor** (pp. 262-269)
  - Mediazione tra musicale/scientifico/tecnologico
  - Lavoro hands-on: programmazione, configurazione tecnologica
  - Contributo creativo sostanziale ma non riconosciuto
  - "Technical realization" = contribution to sound materials

- **Questione dell'authorship** (pp. 265-269)
  - Compositori dipendenti da tutors (tranne poche eccezioni)
  - Tutors fanno "il lavoro sporco"
  - Copyright: composer + IRCAM (tutor escluso)
  - Tutor pagato come "official for technical labor"
  - Casi estremi: pezzi che "suonano come il tutor"

  - Lavoro non pagato per compositori visiting

## 10. CITAZIONI CHIAVE PER LA TESINA

### Sul potere e l'istituzione
**p. 89**
> "Officials spoke cynically of IRCAM as 'official art' because of the dominance of Boulez's personality and aesthetic, whereas the small centers were considered more free, anarchic, and open: 'they have no art directors.' IRCAM was seen as institutionalizing and so as smothering creative individuality."

**p. 143**
> "On the surface, IRCAM was in 1984 a highly bureaucratized institution. With written statutes, a hierarchy of departments and salaried officials, an elaborate management structure, and plentiful documentation and memos, the institute appeared both rule-bound and routinized. Centralized French bureaucracy penetrated the very language used."

### Sulla tecnologia
**pp. 184-185**
> "One of the more or less implicit principles of IRCAM's dominant ideology at this time was a hostility and contempt toward all commercial developments and especially 'low-tech' or small consumer technologies. Consistent with Boulez's own ideology, it was held that IRCAM had nothing to learn from commerce."

**p. 186**
> "The 4X project originated when the designer, physicist BU, was brought to IRCAM in 1975 by the composer Berio who, the story goes, wanted 'a real-time synthesizer with a thousand oscillators.'"

### Sul lavoro e lo sfruttamento
**p. 141-142**
> "Thus IRCAM's vanguard musicians and researchers were tempted into self-exploitation through collusion with an ideology in which, for the present, cultural and economic capital were inversely correlated: high cultural status accrued to those who were seen to exploit themselves the most and whose economic position vis-a-vis the 'established order' was weakest."

**p. 267**
> "Often, working with unskilled composers, tutors joked cynically between themselves that the musical results came out uncannily similar to the tutor's own sounds and music."

### Sul modernismo vs postmodernismo
**p. 302-303**
> "When we scrutinize aurally the IRCAM music of IRCAM's young postmodernists from this period it becomes clear, above all, how dissimilar it is not only to mainstream popular music but also to the non-IRCAM postmodern experimental tradition: composers such as Glass, Reich, Nyman, Rzewski."

### Sul finanziamento
**p. 85-86**
> "In 1978, IRCAM's subsidy was 40 percent and the EIC's 30 percent of the total state budget for contemporary music. IRCAM's preeminence was also expressed in its greater scale, the unusual administrative autonomy and legal status it was granted, and, compared with the mainly national scope of the other centers, its internationalism."

## 11. SCHEMA COMPARATIVO IRCAM vs DIY (da completare con tue fonti DIY)

### IRCAM (Europa - Francia)
- **Istituzionalizzazione**: massima, statale
- **Finanziamento**: pubblico centralizzato (30M francs/anno)
- **Tecnologia**: high-tech, custom, esclusiva (4X, Chant)
- **Organizzazione**: verticale, gerarchica, burocratica
- **Divisione lavoro**: specializzazione estrema, sfruttamento tutors
- **Accesso**: elitario, commissioni selezionate
- **Ideologia**: razionalismo, scientismo, modernismo
- **Relazione con commercio**: disprezzo, rifiuto
- **Base sociale**: élite culturale, stato francese
- **Output**: pezzi unici, highly theorized

### DIY Americano (da tue fonti)
- **Istituzionalizzazione**: minima, autonoma
- **Finanziamento**: autoprodotto, performance, piccoli grants
- **Tecnologia**: low-tech, bricolage, surplus militare, accessibile
- **Organizzazione**: orizzontale, collettiva, decentralizzata
- **Divisione lavoro**: compositori = costruttori = performers
- **Accesso**: aperto, community-based
- **Ideologia**: empiricismo, artigianato, sperimentalismo
- **Relazione con commercio**: pragmatica, talvolta necessaria
- **Base sociale**: scene alternative, university labs, art colleges
- **Output**: performance-based, instrument-centric
